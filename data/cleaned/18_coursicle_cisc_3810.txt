DESCRIPTION:
3 hours; 3 creditsIntroduction to database systems. Comparison to file processing systems. Data models. Relational, hierarchical, and network systems. Database design. Normal forms. Study of several real-world database management systems, with an emphasis on microcomputer applications. Database recovery query and transaction processing, concurrency. Distributed and object-oriented databases. Prerequisite: Computer and Information Science 3130 [22]. Enrollment Requirements: COMPUTER AND INFORMATION SCIENCE 3130 [22] WITH A GRADE OF C OR HIGHER IS REQUIRED TO TAKE THIS COURSE. Requirement Designation: Regular Liberal Arts.
